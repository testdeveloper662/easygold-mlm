1.0 PURPOSE AND BACKGROUND
1.1 Business of the Company
1.1.1 The Company is engaged in the business of offering structured investment opportunities to the public, including an investment product known as “GoldFlex” or “Gold Flex,” which is designed around asset-backed investment participation in gold and precious metals in accordance with applicable Nigerian laws and regulatory guidelines.
1.1.2 The Company, in the course of expanding its investor base and market reach, desires to engage independent introducers to identify and introduce potential investors who may be interested in subscribing to the GoldFlex product.
1.2 Role of the Broker
1.2.1 The Broker represents that it has relationships, networks, and access to potential investors and is willing to introduce such persons to the Company strictly in the capacity of an independent introducer.
1.2.2 The Parties expressly acknowledge that the Broker is not being engaged as a partner, agent, or representative of the Company but solely as an independent contractor whose role is limited to introductions.
2.0 APPOINTMENT AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
2.1 Appointment
2.1.1 The Company hereby appoints the Broker, and the Broker hereby accepts the appointment, to act as a non-exclusive Broker/Introducer for the purpose of introducing prospective investors to the Company’s GoldFlex product during the term of this Agreement.
2.1.2 This appointment does not confer exclusivity, territory rights, or guaranteed minimum compensation on the Broker.
2.2 Nature of Services
2.2.1 The Broker’s services under this Agreement shall be strictly limited to identifying prospective investors and facilitating initial contact between such prospective investors and the Company.
2.2.2 An “Introduction” shall be deemed effective where the Broker provides the Company with the identity and contact details of a prospective investor not previously known to the Company and where such introduction results in direct communication between the Company and the prospective investor.
2.2.3 The Broker shall not participate in negotiations, structure investment terms, alter documentation, collect funds, issue receipts, or make commitments on behalf of the Company.
2.3 Company’s Discretion
2.3.1 The Company shall retain full and absolute discretion to determine whether to engage, accept, or reject any prospective investor introduced by the Broker.
2.3.2 The Company shall not be obligated to give reasons for rejecting any prospective investor.
3.0 NON-EXCLUSIVITY AND CONFLICT MANAGEMENT
3.1 Non-Exclusive Relationship
3.1.1 The Parties agree that this Agreement is non-exclusive.
3.1.2 The Company may appoint other brokers, introducers, or agents, and the Broker may render similar services to other entities, provided there is no conflict of interest or misuse of Confidential Information.
3.2 Conflict of Interest
3.2.1 The Broker shall promptly disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest that may arise in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.
3.2.2 The Company reserves the right to restrict or terminate the Broker’s activities where a conflict poses regulatory, reputational, or legal risk.
4.0 INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
4.1 No Employment or Agency
4.1.1 The Broker is engaged as an independent contractor and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employment, partnership, joint venture, fiduciary, or agency relationship.
4.1.2 The Broker shall not represent itself to any third party as having authority to bind or act for the Company.
4.2 Taxes and Statutory Obligations
4.2.1 The Broker shall be solely responsible for all taxes, levies, and statutory deductions arising from commissions earned under this Agreement.
4.2.2 The Company shall bear no responsibility for the Broker’s tax compliance.
5.0 REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND LIMITATIONS
5.1 No Investment Advisory Role
5.1.1 The Broker represents that it is not licensed as an investment adviser, broker-dealer, fund manager, or issuing house and shall not perform any activity requiring such licensing.
5.1.2 The Broker shall not provide financial, investment, or legal advice to prospective investors.
5.2 Compliance with Nigerian Law
5.2.1 The Broker shall conduct its activities in compliance with all applicable Nigerian laws, including regulations issued by the Securities and Exchange Commission.
5.2.2 The Broker shall immediately notify the Company of any inquiry or investigation by any regulatory authority relating to its activities under this Agreement.
6.0 COMMISSION AND COMPENSATION
6.1 Entitlement to Commission
6.1.1 The Broker shall be entitled to a commission equal to five percent (5%) of the gross amount actually received by the Company from an investor validly introduced by the Broker.
6.1.2 Commission shall only accrue upon actual receipt of cleared funds by the Company.
6.2 Payment Timeline
6.2.1 Commission earned shall be paid within ten (10) business days after the Company receives the investment funds.
6.3 Tail Period
6.3.1 The Broker shall remain entitled to commission on investments made by introduced investors within eighteen (18) months after termination of this Agreement, provided the introduction occurred during the subsistence of this Agreement.
7.0 CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION
7.1 Confidential Information
7.1.1 All proprietary, financial, commercial, and operational information disclosed under this Agreement shall constitute Confidential Information.
7.1.2 Confidential Information shall not be disclosed or used for any purpose outside this Agreement.
7.2 Survival
7.2.1 Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination of this Agreement for three (3) years, or indefinitely where required by law.
8.0 REPRESENTATIONS, WARRANTIES, AND UNDERTAKINGS
8.1 Mutual Representations
8.1.1 Each Party represents that it has full legal capacity and authority to enter into this Agreement.
8.1.2 Each Party warrants that execution of this Agreement does not violate any existing obligation.
9.0 INDEMNITY
9.1 Mutual Indemnity
9.1.1 The Broker shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company, its directors, officers, employees, affiliates, and agents (collectively, the “Company Parties”) from and against all losses, damages, liabilities, claims, demands, fines, penalties, and expenses (including legal and professional fees) arising from any act or omission of the Broker, including but not limited to:

a. breach of this Agreement;

b. negligence, misrepresentation, or fraud;

c. violation of any applicable law, regulation, or regulatory guideline;

d. unauthorized representations made to clients, investors, or third parties; and

e. any act that exposes the Company Parties to regulatory, financial, or reputational risk.

9.1.2 The Company shall have no obligation to indemnify the Broker except where losses are solely caused by the Company’s gross negligence or willful misconduct, and provided that such losses are not contributed to by the Broker.
9.1.3 The indemnity obligations under this Clause shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.
10.0 LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
10.1 Limitation
10.1.1 The Broker acknowledges that under no circumstances shall the Company be liable to the Broker for any indirect, special, incidental, or consequential loss, including loss of profit, business opportunity, or reputation.
10.1.2 The total liability of the Company to the Broker for any claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed the total commissions actually paid to the Broker as at the date the claim arises.
10.1.3 The Company shall not be liable for any loss arising from the Broker’s failure to comply with laws, regulatory directives, or internal Company policies.
10.1.4 Nothing in this Clause shall limit liability for fraud, gross negligence, or wilful misconduct by the Company.
11.0 TERM AND TERMINATION
11.1 Term
11.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of one (1) year, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Clause.
11.1.2 Renewal of this Agreement shall only occur by express written agreement of the Company. The Company reserves the right to refuse renewal without any liability to the Broker.
11.2 Termination
11.2.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement for convenience at any time by giving thirty (30) days’ written notice.
11.2.2 The Company may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect upon written notice if:

a. the Broker commits a material breach of this Agreement or any applicable law, regulation, or policy;

b. the Broker acts in a manner that exposes the Company to regulatory, financial, or reputational risk;

c. the Broker is insolvent, bankrupt, or subject to any winding-up or similar proceedings.

11.2.3 Termination shall be without prejudice to any accrued rights or liabilities of the Company, including claims for indemnity, outstanding commissions, or damages arising prior to termination.
12.0 DISPUTE RESOLUTION
12.1 Scope of Disputes
12.1.1 Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Broker Agreement, including disputes relating to client solicitation, representations or disclosures to investors, commission payment, suspension, forfeiture or claw-back of commissions, regulatory or compliance breaches, indemnity claims, termination, or post-termination obligations (a “Dispute”), shall be resolved in accordance with this Clause.
12.1.2 Disputes arising from investor complaints, regulatory inquiries, or liabilities attributable to the acts or omissions of the Broker in the performance of this Agreement shall fall within the scope of this Clause.
12.2 Negotiation
12.2.1 The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any Dispute through good faith negotiations.
12.2.2 A Party shall notify the other in writing of the Dispute, and the Parties shall engage in discussions within fourteen (14) days and seek to resolve the Dispute within thirty (30) days, failing which either Party may proceed to arbitration.
12.3 Arbitration
12.3.1 Any Dispute not resolved under Clause 12.2 shall be finally resolved by arbitration.
12.3.2 The arbitration shall be conducted in accordance with the Arbitration and Mediation Act, 2023, which is deemed incorporated into this Agreement.
12.3.3 The arbitral tribunal shall consist of a sole arbitrator, jointly appointed by the Parties or, failing agreement within fourteen (14) days, appointed in accordance with the Act.
12.3.4 The seat of arbitration shall be Lagos State, Nigeria, the language shall be English, and the arbitral tribunal may grant any relief available to a court of competent jurisdiction in Nigeria.
12.3.5 The costs of arbitration shall be borne as determined by the arbitral tribunal.
12.4 Confidentiality
12.4.1 The arbitration proceedings and award shall be confidential, except where disclosure is required by law, a regulatory authority, or for enforcement purposes.
12.5 Finality
12.5.1 The arbitral award shall be final and binding on the Parties and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction in Nigeria.
12.0 GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
12.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.
12.2 The courts of Lagos State shall have exclusive jurisdiction.
