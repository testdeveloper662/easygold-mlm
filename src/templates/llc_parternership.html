1. Engagement and Scope of Services
1.1 Services. Company hereby engages Partner, and Partner agrees, to act as a non-exclusive introducer of potential investors to Company (the “Services”). Partner’s Services are limited to identifying and introducing to Company prospective investors who may have an interest in investing in Company or its projects. Partner shall not have authority to negotiate or conclude any investment or transaction on behalf of Company, but will facilitate initial contact and meetings between interested investors and Company.
1.2 Introductions. An “Introduction” under this Agreement shall occur when Partner provides Company with the name and contact information of a prospective investor not already known to or in contact with Company, and arranges initial communication between Company and such investor. The Parties may mutually pre- approve in writing each prospective investor to be introduced by Partner under this Agreement to avoid duplication or conflicts. Company reserves the right, in its sole discretion, to accept or reject any potential investor introduced by Partner.
1.3 Non-Exclusivity. The Parties acknowledge and agree that this Agreement is non-exclusive. Company may engage other finders, consultants, brokers, or agents to seek investment, and Partner may perform similar introduction or consulting services for other entities, provided that (a) Partner continues to fulfill its obligations under this Agreement, (b) no Confidential Information (as defined herein) of Company is used or disclosed in breach of this Agreement, and (c) any such other activities do not pose a conflict of interest or breach of Partner’s duties hereunder.
1.4 Independent Efforts. Nothing in this Agreement obligates Company to consummate an investment with any investor introduced by Partner. Company retains sole discretion over whether and on what terms to enter into any investment or transaction with a prospective investor introduced by Partner. Partner’s role is limited to making introductions, and Partner acknowledges that Company may decline to pursue any particular opportunity with an introduced investor for any reason.
2. Independent Contractor Relationship
2.1 Independent Contractor. Partner is engaged by Company as an independent contractor for the sole purpose of performing the Services described in this Agreement. No employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship is created by this Agreement, and neither Party shall hold itself out as an employee, agent, affiliate, or representative of the other. Partner has no authority to bind Company to any contract, commitment, or obligation, and shall not represent to any third party that Partner has any such authority.
2.2 No Benefits; Taxes. As an independent contractor, Partner shall not be eligible for, nor entitled to, any benefits that Company provides to its employees (such as salary, insurance, paid leave, or retirement benefits). Partner is solely responsible for all taxes, withholdings, and other statutory or contractual obligations of any sort (including, without limitation, income tax, Social Security, Medicare, self-employment taxes, unemployment insurance, and workers’ compensation coverage) with respect to the compensation received under this Agreement. No withholding will be made by Company for any taxes or contributions; all such taxes and charges are the sole responsibility of Partner, and Partner shall file and pay them as required by law. Partner shall indemnify and hold Company harmless from any claims, liabilities, or penalties imposed on Company for failure to withhold or pay any taxes or fees on amounts paid to Partner, as further provided in Section 11.
2.3 Control of Work. Partner shall retain sole and absolute discretion in the manner and means of carrying out the Services, subject to the terms of this Agreement. Partner will use Partner’s own tools, resources, and work methods, and is free to arrange Partner’s work schedule; however, Partner agrees to perform the Services in a professional manner consistent with industry standards and any guidelines provided by Company. Partner is not required to work exclusively for Company and may be engaged in other business activities, provided that such activities do not conflict or interfere with the performance of Services under this Agreement.

4. No Investment Advisor or Broker-Dealer Status
4.1 No Broker-Dealer or Advisor. Partner represents and warrants that Partner is not a registered brokerdealer, broker, dealer, investment banker, financial advisor, or investment adviser, and is not an associated person of a broker-dealer, under U.S. federal or state securities laws. Partner is entering into this Agreement on the express condition that the Services (introductions of potential investors) do not require such registration or licensing. Partner shall not perform any act or provide any service hereunder for which such a license or registration is required. Specifically, Partner will not engage in any negotiations of investment terms, provide any investment recommendation or advice to any investor or Company, solicit or handle funds or securities, and will not perform any other activities that would require registration as a broker, dealer, or investment adviser under applicable law.
4.2 Finder’s Capacity. The Parties agree and acknowledge that Partner’s role is solely that of a “finder” or introducer. In providing the Services, Partner will act strictly in a limited capacity to introduce Company to prospective investors with whom Partner has pre-existing relationships, in exchange for the compensation set forth herein. Partner shall not market or offer any securities of Company for sale to the public, and shall not hold itself out to any person as having authority to make representations on behalf of Company beyond those made in written materials approved by Company. It is understood that Partner is not a licensed broker-dealer or an investment banking firm and shall not act in such a capacity. All investment decisions and securities transactions will be negotiated and consummated directly between Company and the investor (or through Company’s designated professionals), and nothing in this Agreement contemplates Partner’s participation in the offer, sale, or purchase of securities beyond the initial introduction.
4.3 No Investment Advice or Recommendations. Partner will not provide any financial, legal, or investment advice to any prospective investor as part of the Services. Partner shall not make any representations regarding the merits or potential future performance of Company or any investment, and shall not recommend or urge any particular investment decision to any prospective investor. All Parties acknowledge that each prospective investor must rely solely on the Company’s own information and disclosures and on the investor’s own due diligence, analysis, and advisors for any investment decision. Partner may provide prospective investors with Company-approved materials or information expressly authorized by Company for dissemination, but Partner shall not add any commentary, opinion, or analysis regarding any such opportunity that might be construed as investment advice. Each Party hereto is responsible for its own decisions and should seek independent professional advice as needed.
4.4 Regulatory Compliance. The Parties intend to conduct their relationship and any introductions in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including U.S. federal and state securities laws. Company represents that it will conduct any offering or sale of securities in compliance with an applicable exemption from registration (such as a private placement under Regulation D, etc.), and Company will not knowingly violate any securities laws in accepting investments from investors introduced by Partner. Partner agrees to comply with all applicable laws in performing the Services and will not take any action that would jeopardize the availability of any securities law exemption or cause either Party to be in violation of law. In particular, if the Company’s offering is a private placement, Partner shall not engage in any form of general solicitation or general advertising not permitted under applicable law. Partner will limit introductions to investors with whom Partner has a substantive pre-existing relationship or whom Partner reasonably believes to be “accredited investors” or otherwise qualified under applicable law, as instructed by Company. Partner shall immediately notify Company if Partner is approached by any regulator or other authority regarding any aspect of this Agreement or the introductions made hereunder.
4.5 No Illegal Payments. Partner and Company each represent that no payment or compensation to be provided under this Agreement is in return for any illegal or unethical act, and neither Party will, directly or indirectly, offer, pay, solicit, or accept any money or anything of value as a bribe, kickback, or other improper influence in connection with any investment. Any compensation paid to Partner hereunder is solely for the introduction Services and not for the actual purchase or sale of any security.
5. Compensation (Commission)
5.1. Success Fee/Commission. As compensation for the Services, Company shall pay Partner a commissionbased success fee (the “Commission”) for each Successful Investment that is made by an investor introduced by Partner. A “Successful Investment” means that during the Term of this Agreement or within the applicable Tail Period after termination (as defined below), Company receives a capital investment (whether equity, debt, or other financing as agreed) from an Introduced Investor (defined as a prospective investor introduced by Partner under this Agreement who was not already in Company’s pipeline or known to Company prior to introduction). The Commission shall be calculated as follows:
a.
Commission Rate. Partner shall be entitled to a commission equal to % (5 percent) of the total gross amount of capital investment actually received by Company from the Introduced Investor as part of the initial investment transaction. (For example, if an Introduced Investor invests $1,000,000 in Company, Partner would earn a commission of $50.000) The specific percentage may vary for different investors or transactions only if agreed in writing by the Parties in advance of the investment.
a.
Success Event. The Commission is deemed earned by Partner only upon the closing of a transaction in which Company receives the investment funds from an Introduced Investor (or, if the investment involves multiple closings or tranches, as each closing occurs, proportional to the funds received at that closing). No Commission is due for introductions that do not result in an actual investment in Company. For clarity, mere introduction alone does not trigger any payment; the compensation is strictly contingent upon the Company’s receipt of investment capital from an Introduced Investor as a direct result of the Partner’s introduction.
a.
Payment Timing. Company shall pay the earned Commission to Partner promptly following the closing and receipt of funds for a Successful Investment. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, payment of the Commission shall be due within 10 business days after Company’s receipt of the investment funds. The Commission shall be paid by wire transfer or other immediately available funds to an account designated by Partner, after receipt of an invoice or payment request from Partner (if customarily required by Company’s accounts payable procedures).
a.
Multiple Closings or Follow-on Investments. If an Introduced Investor makes multiple investments or follow-on investments in Company (e.g., in subsequent financing rounds) during the Term or Tail Period, those subsequent investments shall also be considered in determining additional Commission owed to Partner, unless such investor was separately introduced by another party for the subsequent transaction or the Parties agree otherwise in writing. Unless otherwise agreed, the same Commission Rate shall apply to any subsequent investment by the same Introduced Investor consummated within the Tail Period or while this Agreement is in effect.
5.2. Tail Period. In order to protect Partner’s interest in introductions made, the Parties agree that if this Agreement expires or is terminated for any reason, Partner shall still be entitled to the Commission for any investment made by an Introduced Investor within eighteen (18) months after the termination or expiration of the Agreement (the “Tail Period”), provided that the initial introduction of such investor by Partner to Company occurred during the Term of this Agreement. In other words, Company’s obligation to pay Commissions to Partner with respect to any particular Introduced Investor shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of 18 months. If an investment from an Introduced Investor is consummated during such Tail Period, it will be deemed a Successful Investment and Partner shall be paid the applicable Commission as set forth in this Section 5, as though this Agreement were still in effect. However, no Commission shall be payable to Partner under this provision if: (a) prior to Partner’s introduction, Company was already in bona fide negotiations or discussions with the investor about a potential investment (as evidenced by written records) or had previously been introduced to the investor by a third party; or (b) the investment by the investor is not in any way attributable to Partner’s introduction or efforts.
5.3. Expenses. Partner shall bear all of Partner’s own costs and expenses in performing the Services (including travel, lodging, meals, communications, office expenses, etc.), unless otherwise agreed in writing in advance by Company. No expenses shall be reimbursed by Company except if Company has expressly authorized a specific expense in writing. Any approved reimbursable expenses shall be documented and submitted to Company in accordance with Company’s expense reimbursement policies for payment
5.4. No Other Compensation. Except for the Commission and any pre-approved expense reimbursements explicitly provided for in this Agreement, Partner shall not be entitled to any other compensation, fees, retainers, or remuneration of any kind from Company or from any Introduced Investor. Partner acknowledges that the Commission is an all-inclusive fee for the Services and covers any and all efforts by Partner to make introductions regardless of time, effort, or expense incurred, except as may be separately agreed for expense reimbursement. Partner is responsible for all of its own costs of doing business as described in Section 2.2 above.
5.5. Full Disclosure of Compensation. Partner shall, if required by law or regulation, disclose to any prospective investor that Partner will receive compensation from Company in the form of a success fee or commission in the event the investor invests in Company. The Parties agree to cooperate in good faith to structure and implement any required disclosures to investors or regulators about this compensation arrangement to ensure compliance with the law (for example, under SEC Rule 506(b)(4) or similar rules requiring disclosure of certain transaction-based compensation to “solicitors”). Partner shall not misrepresent the nature of its compensation to any third party.

The Receiving Party agrees not to use Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party for any purpose except for fulfilling its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement, and not to disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except to such of its own directors, officers, employees, agents, or advisors who have a need to know it for the aforementioned purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement. The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of confidentiality by any person to whom it discloses the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.
6.4. Required Disclosure. If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent permitted by law) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing of such requirement so that the Disclosing Party may seek an appropriate protective order or other remedy. The Receiving Party will reasonably cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking such protection at the Disclosing Party’s expense. If disclosure is still required, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information legally required to be disclosed and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that any Confidential Information so disclosed is accorded confidential treatment.
6.5. Return or Destruction. Upon the written request of the Disclosing Party at any time, and in any event upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return to the Disclosing Party or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s election) all materials in any form embodying or reflecting any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information that the Receiving Party still maintains or controls, and shall certify in writing to the Disclosing Party its compliance with the foregoing (provided that the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information as required by law or for routine backup and record-keeping purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations).
6.6. Confidentiality of Agreement. Each Party agrees not to disclose the existence or terms of this Agreement to any third party without the prior written consent of the other Party, except (i) to such Party’s legal, financial, or tax advisors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality, (ii) as required by law or regulatory authority (in which case, to the extent practicable, the disclosing Party shall inform the other Party in advance), or (iii) in confidence in connection with an actual or potential merger, acquisition, or sale of assets or business (including due diligence related thereto). The Parties acknowledge that maintaining the confidentiality of this Agreement is a material consideration and part of their mutual undertaking.
6.7. Survival. The provisions of this Section 6 shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years from the date of such termination or expiration, or for so long as the Confidential Information remains a trade secret under applicable law, whichever is longer. Notwithstanding the foregoing, any investor lists or personal data that may be exchanged should be treated in accordance with any applicable privacy laws indefinitely, and Company’s obligations to not disclose Partner’s compensation except as required by law shall survive indefinitely.
